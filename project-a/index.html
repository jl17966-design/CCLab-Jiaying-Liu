[ 🐱Meow-Rain🌧 ]

Scientific Name:

Felis Pluvia Interpolatus

Discovered by / at:

Jiaying Liu, a rainy night in Digital Jade Dragon Snow Mountain

Family:

Interpolated Aquatic Feline (A branch of the Generative Raindrop family characterized by high agility and mood-based color shifts).

Habitat:

Born: Rainy nights in mountainous areas Live: In the sky

Appearance:

A raindrop-like creature. Its head features two observant black eyes that track movement with precision. It typically glows with a white hue, while approaching, with a calm, light-blue hue. It will also change color when touched and disappear in the surrondings when continuously touched.

Key Characteristics:

  • Like a cat, curious about new things but also afriad of them
  • Can detect people’s approaching
  • Change in speed and color when touched
  • Can blend into the environment in extremely nervous status
  • Upon detecting lightning ('L' key), it breaks into a high-speed, noise-driven escape path to avoid the strike.

What we know so far about [Meow-Rain]? During the rainy nights in the mountainous areas, there lives the Meow-Rain. It is a generative creature, born from the mountain's humidity and the rhythmic pulse of falling water. Meow-Rain possesses a soul like cats—aloof, observant, and fiercely territorial. It views the misty mountains as its sanctuary. When you approach from a distance, it is driven by a quiet curiosity; it tracks your movements with its deep, dark eyes, subtly drifting toward you as if trying to interpret the nature of an uninvited guest. However, the creature is defined by its proud nature. Should you get too close, its internal alarm triggers, and it backs away to maintain a respectful distance. Its survival instinct is most evident during direct interaction: when "touched" (clicked), it harmonizes its frequency with the atmosphere, blending itself into the environment's hue until it becomes a mere ghost in the fog. But nothing shatters its grace like the crack of thunder. When the lightning strikes (the 'l' key is pressed), it abandons its post and darts through the mountains in a “noise” path, desperate to outrun the lightning.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature

    1. The Meow-Rain is naturally curious but socially anxious. To initiate an interaction, maintain your cursor at 50–800 pixels.
  • The Response: You will notice its body subtly stretching toward you. Its eyes will lock onto your position. This is the "Curiosity Phase" where the creature is attempting to catalog your presence.
  • Guideline: Moving your cursor in slow, rhythmic circles will often push the creature into a steady oscillation.
    • 2. As you move closer (within 50 pixels), the Meow-Rain’s defensive instincts override its curiosity.
  • The Response: It will begin to accelerate its internal pulse (Speed increases to 0.3). This is a warning sign. If you persist in "chasing" it, it will use its territorial strength to push its center of mass away from yours.
  • Guideline: Avoid rapid movements. High-speed approaches are interpreted as predatory behavior.
    • 3. If you must "touch" the creature (clicking the mouse while on it), be prepared for it to disappear.
  • The Response: The Meow-Rain will blend itself into the environment, rapidly lerping its HSB values to match the mountain's current light.
  • Guideline: Do not be alarmed if it seems to vanish. It is still there, merely "hiding in plain sight" by vibrating at the same frequency as the falling rain. Release the pressure to allow it to reform its light-blue physical manifestation.
